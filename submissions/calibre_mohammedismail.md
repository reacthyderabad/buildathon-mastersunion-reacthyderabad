## Project Description

**Calibre** is a clean dashboard that monitors how much money an organization loses to unnecessary meeting overhead. It is built for founders, HR managers, and finance teams who know their teams are stuck in endless calendar syncs but don't know the actual financial cost to the business.

Instead of unreliable manual timesheets, Calibre parses raw calendar descriptions to map meetings to projects automatically. It checks the attendee list, matches them to internal salary tiers behind the scenes, and calculates exact meeting costs in real time. This lets leadership pinpoint cost leaks and see where engineering time goes, without ever exposing individual salary figures on screen.

---

## Approach

The approach behind Calibre was to solve a very real workplace problem: companies spend a lot of money in meetings, but they rarely know the actual cost. Instead of depending on manual time tracking, the product focuses on using raw calendar data as the source of truth.

A simple flow was designed where the system reads meeting titles, descriptions, duration, and attendee lists, then connects that information to projects and internal salary bands. AI is used to classify meetings and predict which project or team they belong to. This makes the dashboard useful because it turns everyday calendar activity into cost insights that leadership can actually act on.

The dashboard is able to process meeting data, classify meetings by project, and show estimated meeting costs. The cost summary view and meeting breakdown sections are also working. Privacy is maintained by hiding individual salary details while still showing total meeting expense.

## What is Still in Progress?
The meeting classification accuracy is still being improved, especially for unclear or short meeting titles. Better salary band mapping, recurring meeting analysis, and a more interactive dashboard experience are also in progress. Calendar integrations and exportable reports still need refinement.

## Challenges Faced

One of the biggest challenges was estimating meeting cost in a way that stays useful for the company without exposing private salary information. Another challenge was handling vague meeting names and still mapping them correctly to the right project or team. It was also important to present financial insights in a simple and non-confusing dashboard.
